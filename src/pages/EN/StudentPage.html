Presentation

The computer science departments of France's IUTs train students in computer science and teamwork in order to integrate them into professional life.

They offer training on a human scale, supervised by available professors, as well as by experienced professionals who provide students with practical and concrete experience in the IT professions.

Teaching in computer science (DUT or Professional license) is based on 3 axis, each of them requiring specific qualities and skills:

Analysis and design :

Preliminary work in relation with the customer to specify his needs and expectations and the design of a computer processing scheme.

Communication, open-mindedness, capacity for analysis and synthesis ...

Algorithms and programming :

Effective implementation of processing up to coding.

Logical spirit, technical precision ...

System and network architecture :

Operation of a computer, its operating system and networks (Internet, Intranet, ...).

Curiosity, patience ...

In addition to these targeted courses, half of the general education disciplines are added (Mathematics, Economics and Management, English and Communication, etc.). This gives the course a multidisciplinary aspect.

furthermore, the evaluation is not only based on theory but also on real-life situations, via tutored projects and an internship in a company, enabling the knowledge and skills acquired during the training to be validated.

The IUT

The University Institutes of Technology in France are spread throughout the country and number 115, which ensures a strong anchoring in the local and regional economic environment. Meetings between department directors of the IUTs ensure the national coherence of education.

The IUT has a quality of university education recognized and plebiscited by companies for more than 40 years, thanks to the implementation of programs adapted to the professional reality and the creation of diplomas responding to the emergence of new professions.

The courses covering most sectors (secondary and tertiary) and offering the collaboration of professionals are the strength and success of the IUTs, and in fact allow students to find a job within 6 months after graduation.

This diversification of diplomas and courses leads to the implementation of different pedagogical methods, which are thus suitable for a greater number of students and professionals wishing to return to school or do a validation of acquired experience (VAE).

Furthermore, the IUTs, in partnership with foreign universities, offer study abroad and internships.

Computer Science Department

The teaching of computer science at the IUT gives a large part to the professional dimension while preserving fundamental components; the student thus acquires know-how but also knowledge, basic concepts and working methods that he will be able to use in a professional situation.

The DUT, university and professional diploma (level L2 of the LMD scheme, 120 European credits):

Holders of the DUT in Computer Science are superior technicians, capable of participating in design, the realization and implementation of computer systems. However, if they wish, they can continue their studies after graduation.

The Professional License "Computer Systems and Software" (SIL), diploma of level L3 in the LMD scheme (Bachelor's degree, 180 European credits): these pages present the professional licenses offered in the computer science departments of the IUT.

SIL licensees are specialists capable of participating in a software development project and ultimately taking full responsibility for it.

Validation of acquired knowledge :

It is also possible to have one's experience (professional or non-professional) recognized in order to obtain a diploma, a title or a certificate of professional qualification. You can visit the government site for the validation of acquired experience (VAE) .

Testimonies

Testimony of Damien Lefebvre

With his scientific high school diploma in his pocket, Damien Lefebvre spent a few months in university, before reorienting himself in search of a more supervised education. His university experience having revealed a strong taste for computer science, he decided to apply to the IUT of Villetaneuse to prepare a DUT in Computer Science with a Software Engineering option in 2002.

The supervision offered by passionate teachers and the acquisition of rigorous work methods encouraged him to complete his training via the Professional Licence in Computer Systems and Software, option Software Engineering, offered by the same IUT.

"The knowledge and skills developed through three years of studying algorithms, databases, but also the accounting aspects of the business world, were the key that enabled me to be recruited by a major French IT services company, after a four-month internship at the end of the year. "

For a year and a half now, the young Programmer Analyst has been putting his skills at the service of major clients, including renowned banking institutions, and wishes to focus on "Consulting" in the future.
